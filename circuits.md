# Circuit Reference — Fictional Formula 1 Game

20 fictional circuits across 6 continents. All circuits are present on the calendar from the start of the game (1930). Locations are real; circuits are entirely fictional.

---

## Season Structure

The season runs in three phases:

- **Opening swing** (Rounds 1–6): Australasia → South America → Asia → East Africa
- **European season** (Rounds 7–14): Belgium through to Finland
- **Closing stretch** (Rounds 15–20): Hong Kong → North America → South Asia → South America → South Africa

## Circuit Profiles — Full Detail

### Round 1 — Australian Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Hawkesbury River Circuit
**Location:** Windsor, New South Wales, Australia
**Lap length:** 6.1 km
**Type:** Parkland / public road
**Character:** Fast, riverside, narrow
**Profile:** Public road circuit following the river bends north-west of Sydney. The flood-plain straight allows very high speeds before a tight riverside chicane. A classic antipodean character — fast, narrow, and unforgiving.

---

### Round 2 — New Zealand Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Marlborough Sounds Circuit
**Location:** Picton, New Zealand
**Lap length:** 5.8 km
**Type:** Parkland / public road
**Character:** Fast, hillside, coastal inlets
**Profile:** Clifftop road circuit above the Marlborough Sounds. Breathtaking scenery throughout; narrow sections with essentially no runoff. The remoteness of the venue is a point of national pride rather than a deterrent.

---

### Round 3 — Gran Premio de Argentina
**Circuit:** Circuito de la Pampa
**Location:** Córdoba, Argentina
**Lap length:** 5.8 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Very fast, flat, windy
**Profile:** Open plains circuit on the pampas outside Córdoba. The pampero wind makes handling treacherous and unpredictable. A distinctly Argentine identity — vast, exposed, and relentless.

---

### Round 4 — Gran Premio de México
**Circuit:** Autodromo de Guadalajara
**Location:** Guadalajara, Mexico
**Lap length:** 4.8 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Medium speed, technical, hot
**Profile:** Guadalajara's purpose-built circuit combines a tight technical stadium section with a long sweeping right-hander. Intense heat and altitude are defining factors. The race is a point of fierce civic pride in Mexico's second city.

---

### Round 5 — 日本グランプリ / Nihon Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Kyushu Motorsport Park
**Location:** Kumamoto, Japan
**Lap length:** 4.4 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Technical, hillside, tight infield
**Profile:** Built into the foothills of the Aso volcanic region. Abrupt changes in gradient punish set-up compromises. The smoking caldera of Mount Aso is visible from the top of the circuit on clear days.

---

### Round 6 — East African Grand Prix
**Circuit:** East African Grand Prix Circuit
**Location:** Nairobi, Kenya
**Lap length:** 5.1 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Medium speed, technical, heat
**Profile:** Kenya's purpose-built circuit sits at high altitude on the outskirts of Nairobi, where the thin air significantly affects engine performance and driver endurance. The East African Grand Prix is the oldest race on the African continent.

---

### Round 7 — Grand Prix de Belgique
**Circuit:** Circuit de Bruxelles-Laeken
**Location:** Brussels, Belgium
**Lap length:** 5.2 km
**Type:** Parkland / estate road
**Character:** Medium speed, park roads
**Profile:** The Belgian Grand Prix uses the perimeter roads of the royal estate parkland at Laeken. A cobblestone hairpin section adds texture and danger in the wet. One of the traditional curtain-raisers of the European season.

---

### Round 8 — Grand Prix de la Côte d'Azur
**Circuit:** Circuit de la Côte d'Azur
**Location:** Nice, France
**Lap length:** 3.1 km
**Type:** Street
**Character:** Fast, narrow, seafront
**Profile:** The Promenade des Anglais forms the thunderous main straight; a tight old-town hairpin brings drivers back through the narrow streets of Nice. The harbour glitters throughout. The jewel of the European season.

---

### Round 9 — Gran Premio di Sicilia
**Circuit:** Autodromo di Palermo
**Location:** Palermo, Sicily, Italy
**Lap length:** 4.2 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Technical, tight, Mediterranean heat
**Profile:** A hillside circuit carved above the Conca d'Oro valley outside Palermo. Tricky gradient changes throughout; the Mediterranean heat builds through the afternoon. Sicily's own grand prix, fiercely independent of the mainland.

---

### Round 10 — Großer Preis von Deutschland
**Circuit:** Schwarzwald-Ring
**Location:** Freiburg im Breisgau, Germany
**Lap length:** 7.1 km
**Type:** Parkland / forest road
**Character:** Fast, sweeping, forest
**Profile:** Long downhill sections plunge through Black Forest pine on the Schwarzwald-Ring. One notorious blind crest — known as der Sprung — has defined the circuit's reputation since its earliest days. The spiritual home of German motor racing.

---

### Round 11 — Velká cena Československa
**Circuit:** Karlovy Vary Kurkurs
**Location:** Karlovy Vary, Bohemia, Czechoslovakia
**Lap length:** 7.4 km
**Type:** Parkland / public road
**Character:** Fast, sweeping, forested valley
**Profile:** The Karlovy Vary Kurkurs follows the wooded banks of the Ohře river before climbing through the spa district's elegant colonnaded streets and out onto a fast hillside loop above the valley. Speeds on the riverside section are among the highest on the European calendar. The grandstands fill with the cream of central European society; the Czech Grand Prix has the atmosphere of a social occasion as much as a sporting one.

---

### Round 12 — British Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Brookthorpe Park Circuit
**Location:** Gloucestershire, England
**Lap length:** 3.3 km
**Type:** Airfield
**Character:** Flat, fast, exposed
**Profile:** Built on a wartime airfield perimeter in the Gloucestershire countryside. Absolutely flat and very fast; the notorious crosswinds off the Severn Vale are an invisible but constant factor. Raw, functional, and unmistakably British.

---

### Round 13 — Svenska Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Göteborg Hamnkurs
**Location:** Gothenburg, Sweden
**Lap length:** 3.9 km
**Type:** Street
**Character:** Medium speed, harbourfront, flat
**Profile:** A dockside street circuit through the Lindholmen and Frihamnen districts of Gothenburg. Tram tracks sealed under the tarmac remain faintly visible and still catch drivers in the wet. Compact but with a genuine overtaking opportunity at the harbour bend.

---

### Round 14 — Suomen Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Rovaniemi Arctic Circuit
**Location:** Rovaniemi, Finland
**Lap length:** 4.7 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Flat, fast, extreme cold, remote
**Profile:** Just south of the Arctic Circle, Rovaniemi hosts the most remote race on the calendar. Permafrost makes resurfacing near-impossible, giving the surface a permanently bumpy character that no set-up can fully tame. Perpetual daylight during summer race weekends adds to the disorienting atmosphere.

---

### Round 15 — Hong Kong Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Central Harbourfront Circuit
**Location:** Central & Wan Chai, Hong Kong
**Lap length:** 3.2 km
**Type:** Street
**Character:** Tight, glamorous, low-speed technical
**Profile:** Colonial waterfront streets wind through Central and Wan Chai with the harbour always visible from the cockpit. Tram tracks sealed beneath the tarmac catch drivers in the wet. The most prestigious race outside Europe — the post-European season showpiece.

---

### Round 16 — Grand Prix du Canada
**Circuit:** Circuit du Lac Meech
**Location:** Gatineau, Quebec, Canada
**Lap length:** 5.5 km
**Type:** Parkland / public road
**Character:** Medium speed, forested, scenic
**Profile:** A national park perimeter road circuit in the Quebec lakeland, surrounded by dense boreal forest. Grip levels shift dramatically with the weather. Quebec's French heritage gives the Canadian Grand Prix a distinctly European atmosphere in a North American wilderness.

---

### Round 17 — American Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Douglas Circuit
**Location:** Roseburg, Oregon, USA
**Lap length:** 5.2 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Fast, sweeping, damp surface, high tyre wear
**Profile:** Carved through old-growth timber on the western Cascade foothills outside Roseburg, the Douglas Circuit runs across sealed volcanic aggregate that stays permanently damp beneath the forest canopy — even in midsummer the tarmac rarely dries completely, and the green-tinged surface at the forest's edge has a treacherous reputation. Tyre wear is the defining story: the abrasive surface destroys rubber faster than almost anywhere on the calendar, and strategies built around softer compounds almost always unravel in the closing stages. The surrounding timber towns send large, raucous crowds; the American Grand Prix draws a different audience from the European parkland races — louder, closer to the barriers, and less forgiving of a poor result.

---

### Round 18 — Punjab Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Lahore Street Circuit
**Location:** Lahore, Pakistan
**Lap length:** 3.7 km
**Type:** Street
**Character:** Slow, technical, cultural
**Profile:** The colonial-era government quarter and the approach roads to the Shalimar Gardens form a tight and technical street circuit through the heart of Lahore. Intense crowd atmosphere; the Punjab Grand Prix is one of the most culturally vivid events on the calendar.

---

### Round 19 — Grande Prêmio do Brasil
**Circuit:** Circuito do Recife Costeiro
**Location:** Recife, Brazil
**Lap length:** 3.8 km
**Type:** Street
**Character:** Slow, technical, humid
**Profile:** Harbourfront and historic centre streets wind through the coastal city of Recife in Brazil's tropical north-east. Extreme heat and humidity are defining factors from the first practice session. The Brazilian Grand Prix in its most raw and elemental form.

---

### Round 20 — South African Grand Prix
**Circuit:** Bushveld Grand Prix Circuit
**Location:** Pretoria, South Africa
**Lap length:** 5.7 km
**Type:** Purpose-built
**Character:** Fast, open, wildlife reserve
**Profile:** A fast and open purpose-built circuit north of Pretoria, set within a game reserve. The surrounding bushveld is a constant presence at the circuit's edge. The season finale — championships are often settled here under the wide South African sky.
